October 12th, 2019 / Suzuran / 12:00 AM

The sun looms over a building in a moderately large city, clouds blocking some of the rays of light into the nearest walls of tan and grey buildings, also generating shadows for where the light doesn’t reach as well. The grass around the building, otherwise referred to as the yards, have a nice lush green color to it, even if most of the way to the entrance is paved with industrial road. The grass and shrubs are regularly tended to, whether by one of the building’s members, or a passing citizen doing community service, be it willingly or forcefully.

This is the city of Suzuran, and this building is their school. To some, it may just be their second home. It’s interior is nothing too special, maybe a handful of posters, but it’s all well put together, even if nothing fancy.

As of the current time, it’s break between one class and the other, specifically a Physics course and Geology as the upcoming one. A few people are out and about in the hallways, checking their schedules, maybe checking themselves out in the mirror, perhaps the occasional gander at a bulletin board.

Homeroom

One room in particular though, the homeroom, has a few people doing their own thing. Their next and previous class have been here anyways, so it wouldn’t make sense to go to another classroom. Since it’s homeroom, it’s a tad more messy and personal. A few stray objects, a whiteboard with some fun drawings and scribbles, some drawers for storage. It gets their work done.

"So...what’s new with you two?" says a red haired girl, sitting in a computer desk, twirling a red and yellow mechanical pencil in her hand. Her hairstyle has twin drills, and a green hair clip to the side. A red tuft of hair on the top, and wearing a school-provided blazer with a grey accent color and a red skirt to match.

Her name is Ringo, part of the Andou family. She’s talking to a rather eager beanie-wearing blonde and a brown haired gal with a ponytail.

"Oh, it’s been great!! I’ve finally caught up with last week’s assignment!" exclaims the blonde. Her name’s Amitie, she’s surely one to talk. Her body language is exaggerated, moving her arms energetically, given her extrovert nature.

"I mean what you’re doing on your own time, Amitie." Ringo replies, chuckling at the end.

"o-Oh! That’s what you meant?"

"Yeah."

"That’s also been going just great!" She can’t help but giggle in her uplifting mood. "I’ve been scrap-booking some more neat goings-on during my walks around the district!" She stretches her arms back.

"Ah, sorry Arle!" stutters Amitie.

"Right, Arle." asks Ringo. "How’s it been with you?"

Arle shrugs it off, albeit carefully staring at her pyramid of cards. "Eh. Could be better. I’m fine with the current load of work, but I’m getting all irritable about it.".

The stack of cards is relatively mediocre compared to other ones Ringo and Amitie have witnessed over the last few months. Some get as high as about half a meter. Folks gossip about the ultra rare ‘1 meter stacks’.

"Maybe that’s why you’re not performing as well."

"You think so?"

"It only makes sense." she replies back with, spinning in the computer desk’s chair rather slowly, pushing the chair with her right leg, the left one following. She portrays a rather unintentionally smug pose, with her eyes closed and a shrug to boot.

"Mph."

Amitie starts staring at the stack of cards, fascinated by how Arle’s been able to do that to kill a few minutes in-between breaks of classes. As she watches Arle carefully stack cards on top of one another, she turns to Ringo, who’s still turning around in the computer desk chair.

"What about you, Ringo?"

The drill-haired stops her spinning to look towards Amitie. "Me?"

"Yeah, silly! What’s on your mind?"

"Hm." She ponders for a moment. "I can’t think of much. The most I’ve done today was work on that assigned project. Besides that, I sorta just hang around with you guys."

"Hah! That’s sweet of you!"

"Aha...yeah." she replies back with, unsure on how to feel about it. "...But still. That’s all. You’ve been into scrap-booking and Arle’s been working on steadying her arms."

"Well, it doesn’t hurt to take a break every now and again." Arle adds in to the conversation, steadily focusing on the cards, not exactly looking at Ringo.

"Yeah!" says Amitie. "You’re free to hang around with us after classes today. I’ll be off on my walk, then I’m off to the shopping district!"

As Amitie goes on about her plans for the evening, Ringo starts to blindly stare into nothing, the conversation between Arle and Amitie fading out to her, not sure what to really respond with. Her expression shows uncertainty.

"...Um."

She quickly snaps herself out of it and turns around to look back at the CRT monitor. It’s displaying her own schoolwork, as the school provides its own service to work on assignments. Despite the time she’s spent on the latest assignment, having been working on a study document for physics (her favorite subject, so to speak), it’s nearing the due date of the 17th, a Saturday. She’s only gotten halfway through with it.

"Ringo?"

"Huh??"

RING!

"Alright then." starts Ringo. "I’ll get the books out."

Homeroom / 3:05 PM

The computer desk appears to still be occupied by Ringo, a few physics book to the right of the mouse, and some stray draft papers to the left of the keyboard, weighed down by a pencil and eraser.

Ringo's currently working with the assignment for the extra hour she has before she actually has to leave the building. Rather fast at that, too. Each stroke on the keyboard has a good amount of pressure to the key where you could hear it from across the room.

Her expression is that of nervousness and determination, both due to stress and wanting to finish something she's spent this much effort on already for the last week.

Through the window, Arle peeks through the window, spotting Ringo over on the left, only barely being obstructed by the frames of the door. Just as she thought, there's Ringo, putting all of her time and effort into a damned document. Unsurprisingly, this bothers Arle.

She knows what to do, however. Much like she said a few minutes earlier with Amitie, just talk to her about it. Simple! She shouldn't mind, right?

The door slowly opens, some force applied so that it doesn't make an obnoxious creaking sound, as the doors are rather worn after years of use. They still serve their function, however.

As she silently stepped over to the desk, Arle took the time to take in the stuff Ringo has laid around on the desk, books and notes, more than that's needed at all for something like this. Especially in her eyes, considering the existence of 'searching it online'.

In a way, she's also kind of impressed with the amount of work Ringo's putting into this. In the event she does get it done in time, it'd definitely get the extra credit. That's besides the point, though.

Ringo hasn't noticed that someone's entered the room at all, rather preferring to keep her attention to the work at hand.

Arle continues to walk towards Ringo until she's just about a meter away from her, bewildered that she hasn't been spotted. She gets close enough to where the shadow of her standing starts to loom over Ringo's workstation.

Ringo notices the new shadow covering her draft papers, and finally notices Arle looking right over her shoulder. She jumps out of her chair with a shriek, nearly stumbling. The knee-jerk reaction gives Arle a jolt too.

"PI!" she yelps out, using a math term rather than something such as 'CHRIST!'. "H-How long have you just been watching over me for?!"

"...About a minute or two."

"Alright, alright, Arle...um. Aren't you...supposed to be with Amitie? Why're you back here? Did you forget something?" Ringo replies, lining up her draft papers to give her hands something to do, now that she's up from her seat.

"That's not why I'm here."

Ringo looks at Arle in confusion. "So why're you here now then?"

Arle struggles to come up with a sentence on the spot, so she gives it a few seconds to put something together.

"It's about you, Ringo."

Ringo's confusion becomes concern. "...What?"

"Erm.. It's about the...project you've been working on since last week."

Ringo's only response is the raise of an eyebrow. Nothing ill-fated, rather not sure what Arle's going on about.

In a quick attempt to put words together like patchwork, Arle does her best not to come off as rude about it, to respect what Amitie suggested to her before she left.

"n-Not that there's anything wrong in of itself for working on something, it's just you've spent... all your time on it? I haven't seen you most of the time since."

Arle's response gives Ringo a bit of a poor feeling. She already knew she hadn't been talking to her and Amitie as of when she started seriously working on the document, but not once had she considered it to concern them. Now that she's thinking about it, she's not sure why she never considered it in the first place. As a conclusion, she gives herself some time to think of what to reply back with.

"I appreciate the concern Arle, believe me, I really do, but I'm sure the both of you know by now that physics has always been a specialty of mine. I wouldn't want that to go to waste if this document's practically only about it."

To Arle, this practically only means 'mind your own damn business'. She knows Ringo well enough that she extends her sentences out often to not come off as rude, but comes off as she's just saying it that way to sound more credible. Can't blame her, though.

Arle starts casually talking about it, thinking less about it past what message she's trying to send over.

"Ringo, if you really think working nonstop over a document for a week, and another 5 days after this is going to benefit you, like, if it's really going to benefit you, at least take some time to rest. I wouldn't be surprised if you start taking all-nighters on this. Hell, I'm pretty sure you've been doing that already."

"That's...still a pretty bad sleep schedule, Ringo. 6 hours of sleep won't do anything."

"Look, you don't see me falling asleep in class, do you?"

"Not downright...but...You've definitely almost done that. Only a few days ago have I had to snap my fingers in front of you so that you'd stay awake for at least another drew minutes till the end of a class."

Arle's hoping that adding stuff that's happened the past week with Ringo will eventually get her to realise her problems and take a breather. She's really had to try and snap Ringo out if it a few days ago, a close call as well.

Ringo puts her arms on the desk, leaning herself down a bit.

"Yeah, well, 'almost' still means 'none' if you round to the lowest tenth."

'This is starting to get ridiculous', Arle thinks to herself.

"It doesn't matter if rounds to none! The fact you were almost sleeping means that the sleep schedule's been screwing you over, and that's because of the document!"

Both sides get increasingly tense in their arguments.

"I'm doing just fine, Arle. You don't need to worry about it, I'll get this done by Saturday."

"That's not what I'm talking about at all!"

Ringo finally starts to get noticeably peeved off at Arle's attempts.

"So WHAT are you getting at??"

Arle can feel something's probably about to happen, but she keeps persisting her main argument.

"I'm just saying you need to take some damn time off!"

Ringo pushes herself.

"I'LL TAKE SOME 'DAMN TIME OFF' WHEN I NEED TO!" exclaims Ringo, her fast approach causes the papers and keyboard to fall off the desk, making a loud clattering sound with some papers slowly descending towards the ground.

The air quickly becomes tense, just as much as their argument's tension. Arle steps back, trying not to have Ringo seemingly run into her. Ringo's breathing had doubled in speed, being more audible as a result.

Arle doesn't know what to reply with at this point, now having Ringo lash out like this. Anything Arle tries to say only comes off as mere stumbles, nothing coherent.

"...Ringo-"

"I'm doing just fine the way I'm handling this, I don't need your damn input on this. When I get this done, you can stop worrying about me constantly, alright?! You've been wasting both your, and my time, so how about YOU give me a DAMN BREAK, and let me work IN PEACE."

3:20 PM. Arle has probably left by now to meet up with Amitie.

chapter 2

Shopping District / 3:20 PM

"What could be taking her so long?" Amitie thinks to herself, checking her phone before stuffing it back in her coat. It's almost been half an hour since she let Arle go talk to Ringo back at the school. Her plans were to check out what was at the district, and then go meet Feli somewhere in the forest. In the meantime, she sits on the closest bench.

Despite this, she still decides to wait for Arle...maybe the two are having a conversation on the matter. She takes this time to really look around the district from where she sits.

The Suzuran shopping district could be considered by one to be where the heart of the town lies, with a large, iconic entrance gate with it's accent green color. While laid out as a regular street, the sides are filled to the brim with stores, majorly community-ran family businesses. Vendors can also be spotted in between.

Some street signs can be found at the crossroads, as the district is rather close to the school. Any nearby shrubs or trees lack their leaves, due to the season. Any remaining leaves are in a shade of orange to a dark brown.

Most buildings here are two stories tall, but some can get up to 4 floors, however not many buildings have a reason to be more than 2 floors tall. The top of them occasionally have a billboard for advertising, but it's not dominant.

Due to the time, it's also starting to get dark out as the sun starts to go below the horizon. The daylight dies out faster at this time of the year, so the streetlights are lit up. The sky has a nice, faded gradient from purple to orange, although the sky is mostly filled with purple.

Amitie has never really took the time to take in these sights for a while now. It's actually pretty calming, seeing this usually peaceful market at this time of day.

Before she knew it, she felt a buzz from her coat's pocket. Upon checking, it's a message from Arle.

"comin to the district now, meet u at the crossroad"

"Oh thank goodness," she says to herself. Arle got through with talking to Ringo. She replies back with a basic "see you there!" which should be more than enough.

She swings her legs over the bench that she's sitting on, excited to start hanging out with Arle for the day at the shopping district. There's been some eye-catching Halloween stuff to celebrate the holiday, and some outfits look like they'd definitely be in her taste.

Suzuran High School / 3:30 PM

"Damn it," she thinks to herself. "Damn it!"

Arle isn't taking the argument she just had with Ringo well. She's only amplified the worries she had to begin with, which is probably the worst way it could have gone. Worst of all, she doesn't know how to break it to Amitie. What's she supposed to say? 'Oh, yeah, she got pissed about it?' That won't do any good.

She checks the rack one by one.. bike... bike... skateboard... bike... aha! Here it is.

Motorcycle.

It looks out of place next to everything else on the rack, but it pretty much does the same thing, and conveniently is able to stick onto the rack without tipping itself over. Plus, if positioned well, two people could be on the motorcycle at a time, which comes in handy, especially with today's trip.

Taking the ignition keys out of her coat pocket, she gets the motorcycle out of the rack and hops on, igniting the engine with a turn of the key. After putting on the helmet hung around the handle of the motorcycle, and a three point turnaround later, she drives off onto the side of the road, quickly making her way to the district.

The engine can be heard sputtering a bit from the cold, but it eventually warms itself into a workable manner. Some people even hear the engine in the classrooms...although they know at this point it's just Arle riding off.
